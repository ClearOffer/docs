Use custom field mapping to translate your Ashby custom fields into ClearOffer fields. When an offer syncs from Ashby, ClearOffer will fill in matching fields automatically.

<Info>
Mappings apply to offers created or updated after you save them. Existing offers aren’t retroactively changed.
</Info>

## Add or edit a mapping

<Steps>
<Step title="Open Integrations → Ashby">
From the Ashby card, click “Show Custom Fields”.
</Step>

<Step title="Add mapping">
Click “Add Mapping”, enter the exact Ashby custom field name, then choose a ClearOffer field from the dropdown.

<Tip>
Field names must match Ashby exactly (including spaces and casing).
</Tip>
</Step>

<Step title="Save">
Click “Save Mapping”. Repeat for each field you’d like to map.
</Step>

<Step title="Edit or delete">
Use the row actions to update or remove a mapping. Deletions require confirmation.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Rules and recommendations

<CardGroup cols={2}>
<Card title="Mapping rules" icon="triangle-exclamation">
- Each ClearOffer field can be mapped at most once  
- Each Ashby field name can appear at most once
</Card>

<Card title="Best practices" icon="lightbulb">
- Use simple, unambiguous Ashby field titles  
- Test with a single offer before rolling out broadly
</Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
Use “Equity Shares (Number)” for numeric equity values. “Base Salary Override” will replace the salary from Ashby.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="My mapping didn’t apply">
- Confirm the Ashby field title exactly matches what’s in Ashby
- Ensure the offer was created/updated after the mapping was saved
- Verify the relevant webhook event (Create/Update) is enabled
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Duplicates prevented">
The UI blocks duplicate Ashby field names and duplicate ClearOffer fields to avoid conflicts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Equity values not recognized">
Ensure “Equity Shares (Number)” is a numeric field in Ashby. Non‑numeric values can’t be imported.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
